No mirrors in the house
Afraid of what he might see



The child's skeleton was mostly intact, laying just by the front gate. There was a NOTICEABLE amount of blood on the gate itself, appearing as a deep brown, having dried and been ATTACKED by the same sand and wind that had begun to form a dune about the nearby remains.

Taran pushed the gate open, being careful not to touch the blood with a sense of dignity that had no place here.

Taran passed the gate and moved, with caution, towards the main house. He did not wanting to disturb or miss anything that might come to be important, and, further, knew the inside would contain much worse than what he had encounted at the gate. Delaying the inevitable is a curious and impractical characteristic of human behaviour.
