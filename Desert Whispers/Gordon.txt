Gordon was a ruthless, unkind and arguably evil man. He went beyond the necessities of a bandit, and ventured well into the realm of cruel. He would go out of his way to kill unnecessarily and often in truly fiendish ways. A long-standing rumour was that he had taken revenge on a man who had beaten him at cards by ____________

This didn't scare Taran so much as discompose him. Rumours are rumours, and, while the macabre rumours surrounding Gordon are surely well exaggerated, taking revenge for losing cards was something more believable, and Taran knew the inconveniences of working with someone whose displeasure at losing was so vitriolic.
